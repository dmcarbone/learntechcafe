## What's the Catch with Most "Free" Blogging Platforms?
These "free" platforms either have:
- A limited-time trial
- Reduced features
- Visually displeasing ads
- Ugly branding you can't remove
- All of the above
In order to remove some of the above features, you likely need to pay to upgrade.

Sure, platroms such WordPress, Wix or Squarespace let you setup a blog with ease. However, with this ease, comes a cost - either a cost in money, performace, or control. Yes, many people use these services. And that's perfectly fine! You can get a professional blog up and running quickly with any one of these services. However...

## What You'll Learn in this Tutorial Series
We'll teach you how to use four main tools to build your blogging website.
1. **Hugo** - A free tatic site generator to create you website and site content (including code)
2. **GitHub** - A place to store your site content and code for free
3. **Netlify** - A service that hosts your site online, for free
4. _Bonus_ How to purchase a custom domain.

> ðŸ¤– _**ZZZT** Purchase a custom domain?_

Yes, A.B.A2, purchase. This is the only piece of the whole puzzle that costs money - if you wish to have a custom domain (like learntechcafe.com), you must purchase it. Luckily, this is optional. If you walk through this process with us, you will be set up with a live, free domain - you just can't customize it unless you buy your own domain. Look for a **Bonus** post at the end of this series for how to set up a custom domain for your blog!

With the above tools, you'll:
- Write your posts in simple Markdown, a basic coding language to make it easy to write site content
- Publish your posts to GitHub
- Automatically deploy your posts to the web, instantly.

It's actually really cool once you get it running!

Yes it takes some learning.
No, it's not drag-and-drop like WordPress.
Don't worry, you won't be required to become a fancy web-develope, knees deep in code!

By the end of this series, you'll have a **fully functional blog** that:
- Loads super fast compared to most paid blog platforms out there
- Doesn't track or advertise to your readers
- Costs you nothing but a domain name (optional)
- Belongs 100% to you
